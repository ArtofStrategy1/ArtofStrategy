Collective Appreciation of Strategy (CAOS): Bridging Perspectives for Success

Collective Appreciation of Strategy (CAOS) is a process used in both military and corporate settings to analyze the situation and environment, identify potential challenges and opportunities, and develop strategies and tactics to achieve a mission or end goal. The process involves bringing together leaders and specialists from different areas to share diverse perspectives and expertise. This helps build a comprehensive understanding of the situation, promotes collaboration and alignment among stakeholders, and enables more informed and effective decisions.

By centralizing diverse perspectives and expertise, organizations can develop plans that are both novel and effective, while ensuring alignment with the organization’s vision, mission, and values. Furthermore, the CAOS process promotes a sense of ownership and buy-in among stakeholders and helps organizations remain agile and responsive to changes in their environment.

Why is Collective Appreciation of Strategy Important?

There are several benefits to conducting collective appreciation of strategy in both military and corporate settings. Key benefits include:

  • Comprehensive Understanding: Bringing together leaders and specialists from different areas allows organizations to develop a complete and nuanced understanding of their internal and external environment. This depth of understanding is similar to why Moltke the Elder personally trained the German General Staff—so that embedded staff officers understood his thinking thoroughly and could guide operations without constantly needing to report back.
  • Diverse Perspectives: Gathering specialists from different fields encourages the sharing of diverse insights and expertise. This often leads to novel solutions to complex problems and the development of innovative strategies and tactics.
  • Collaboration and Alignment: Conducting collective strategy appreciation promotes collaboration among leaders and specialist staff. By involving multiple stakeholders in the planning process, organizations can ensure that everyone shares an understanding of goals, challenges, and opportunities.
  • Ownership and Buy-in: When individuals from different areas are involved in the planning process, they are more likely to feel invested in the resulting strategies and more willing to work together to achieve the organization’s goals.
  • More Effective Decision-making: By analyzing the organization's situation and environment and identifying potential challenges and opportunities, leaders can make decisions that are more likely to be successful and aligned with long-term objectives.
  • Increased Agility: Conducting this process regularly helps organizations stay agile and responsive to environmental changes. By frequently reassessing their situation, organizations can identify emerging issues early and adjust their strategies accordingly.

The CAOS Process

There are several key steps involved in the CAOS process:

  1. Background: Gather background information on the organization’s situation and environment. This may include performance data, market trends, and competitive analysis.
  2. Mission Analysis: Conduct a detailed analysis of the mission or end goal the organization aims to achieve. This includes defining scope, identifying limitations, and determining information gaps.
  3. Strategic Initiatives: Based on mission analysis, develop a set of strategic initiatives or approaches that could be used to achieve the desired outcome. These may arise from brainstorming, market research, or analysis of competitors.
  4. Strategic Courses of Action: Develop specific courses of action that implement the strategic initiatives. These should account for potential challenges and opportunities identified earlier.
  5. Selection: Evaluate and select the best course of action. This may involve guiding principles, benchmarks, and analytical tools such as game theory or cost-benefit analysis.
  6. Evaluation through Wargaming: After selecting a course of action, evaluate its feasibility and effectiveness. Wargaming—simulating different scenarios—can reveal weaknesses or areas for improvement before the plan is implemented.

Military Example of CAOS: The "Window" Technique

During World War II, the British Royal Air Force (RAF) suffered heavy losses due to German radar systems that detected and tracked incoming aircraft. To address this, the RAF brought together specialists from physics, engineering, mathematics, and other fields to develop a new radar-jamming solution.

The result was “Window,” a technique involving clouds of metallic strips dropped from aircraft to confuse radar systems. This collaborative innovation proved highly effective and helped shift the air war in the Allies’ favor.

First used on July 24, 1943, during a raid on Hamburg, Window created hundreds of false radar targets, catching the Germans completely off guard. Its success throughout the remainder of the war showcased the immense value of diverse expertise and collaboration in solving complex strategic challenges.

Conclusion

Collective appreciation of strategy is critical for effective planning and decision-making in both military and corporate environments. By bringing together diverse perspectives, organizations gain a comprehensive understanding of their situation, identify challenges and opportunities, and craft innovative strategies to overcome them. This approach also promotes collaboration, alignment, ownership, and agility—ultimately leading to stronger strategic outcomes.
